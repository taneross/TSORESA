Task 1: Lesson Cycle

In this task, you will assemble a portfolio of evidence that demonstrates your work with students in the context of a single lesson. You will submit a description of the students and your classroom, a lesson overview, a videotape of that lesson citing specific parts for assessors to watch, a commentary on that videotape, all instructional artifacts associated with this lesson, and an analysis of the entire lesson.

What you need to do

1. Choose a lesson that allows you to demonstrate the following:
  • Important content clearly connected to the Ohio Academic Content Standards and your district curricula
  • High expectations for student learning
  • Direct engagement with students during the lesson, allowing you to demonstrate questioning strategies and other varied instructional strategies differentiated as appropriate for student needs
  • The way[s] you use formative assessment strategies as part of the instructional process
  • Your creation of a classroom environment conducive to learning—for example, positive interactions, cognitive challenge, and efficient management of learning activities, pupils, and materials
  • A range of instructional strategies and materials that demonstrate your differentiation of instruction for your particular students’ needs

2. Videotape the lesson, and index the tape using the directions under “Making a Videotape” [Note to Draft (NTD): need to create this doc]

3. Collect all of your instructional artifacts for this lesson. No student work or responses are required.

4. Complete each of the online forms listed above.
